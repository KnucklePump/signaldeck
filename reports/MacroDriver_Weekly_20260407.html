§4 Driver Interactions & Double-Count Prevention

Iran/Hormuz War → US Economic Health
Oil price spike from Hormuz closure would feed into headline CPI, tightening the Fed's policy constraint and weighing on consumer spending. Channel: energy inflation → real income squeeze → consumption contraction.
✓ Resolution: Iran driver assigned the primary oil impact for the short-term. US Economic Health driver captures the transmission effect via CPI/employment lagging indicators. No double-count in energy asset class (scored once under Iran driver, Neutral for US Econ Health).
US-China Tariff War → Structural Deglobalisation
The tariff war is an acute event manifestation of the structural deglobalisation trend. Both point in the same direction for most asset classes (EM Underperform, TIPS Outperform, Gold Outperform).
✓ Resolution: Acknowledged as directionally reinforcing (not double-counting because they affect different time horizons). Tariff War drives Short/Medium signals; Deglobalisation drives Medium/Long signals. Convictions appropriately High for overlapping signals.
US-China Tariff War → China Economic Health
Tariffs reduce China's export receipts, adding to existing property sector pressure. However China is countering with PBOC easing and domestic stimulus. The two forces partly offset at the China equities level.
✓ Resolution: China Equities is scored Neutral in the short-term aggregation (tariff pressure ≈ PBOC stimulus offset). The temporary driver handles the acute trade shock; China enduring driver captures the domestic cycle separately.
Global Monetary Policy → US Fiscal Trajectory
Higher-for-longer rates increase US debt service costs, accelerating fiscal stress. But this interaction is slow-moving and doesn't create near-term double-counting in asset class scores.
✓ Resolution: No short-term double-count. Both drivers independently assign Long Bond as Underperform (supply pressure vs. rate pressure). This reinforcement correctly yields a Underperform net signal for TLT.
